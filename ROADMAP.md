# Some-R-Set — Development Roadmap

## Shipped (production: somerset-scorepad.vercel.app)
- Single-game score pad: bidding/trump entry (two-step), set penalty (subtract bid, can go negative), shoot-the-moon win rule, clockwise dealer rotation with seat diagram, editable player/seat names with quick-add from the (editable) per-device name book, New Game.
- **Game History** — completed games archived with rosters, winner, score, date; tap to expand the hand-by-hand log; delete entries; export/import a JSON backup (dedup on re-import, so it's safe to re-import the same file). Grouped by day, with each day's tournament matches further collapsed under their own tournament — collapsed rows show a summary (games played, per-team win/loss record, champion).
- **Tournament mode**
  - Player-count picker (even players, min 6) → name entry with Quick-add name book (per device, editable, deduped).
  - Random team draw (2 per team); odd team counts allowed (random byes auto-advance).
  - **Single Elimination** bracket with flowchart diagram; pick winner manually or play in-app.
  - **Double Elimination** format (winners + losers brackets, grand final).
  - **Round Robin** format (standings table + schedule grid).
  - Per-match games (concurrent, independent); play/resume a match, winner auto-advances and archives to History.
  - Rematch (same teams) / redraw (same players) at tournament end.
- **Best-of Series mode (4 players / 2 teams).** A lightweight alternative to a bracket: enter 4 names, split into 2 teams (random draw or pick partners), choose Best of 3 / 5 / 7, and play games until a team clinches the majority. Live scoreboard (game wins + game-by-game list), each game played/resumed in-app and archived to History, undo the most recent game, rematch/redraw at the end. Reuses the tournament match-play, locking, sync, and History machinery (`format: "series"`), so it syncs across devices via the same join-code flow.
- **Shared backend (multi-device tournaments)** via Firebase Realtime Database — live simultaneous logging across phones, siloed per tournament, joined via a short friendly join code. Per-device name book stays local.
- **Self-identify as a player.** After starting/joining a synced tournament, tag yourself with your roster name (or change it later from the sync bar). Every match you're part of is auto-archived to your own device's History as it completes, even matches a teammate plays/records — deduped so it's never added twice.
- **Share a casual game, not just a tournament.** The Game tab's solo score pad can now be shared live via the same join-code flow as a tournament — it wraps the current game in a single-game "series" behind the scenes (`startGameShare`) so it reuses the tournament's sync, per-hand locking, and rematch/best-of-N follow-up machinery. A unified **Options** menu (replacing the old standalone "New Game" button) adapts to context: New Game, Share this game, Join with code, Share code, Playing as…, End/Leave.
- **Viewer perspective on the Game tab.** Once you identify as one of the four named players ("Playing as…"), the seat table and score totals reorder so your seat/team render first, and team labels adapt to your point of view — "We"/"They" while team names are untouched defaults (correct regardless of which real team index you're actually on), a team's real custom name once it's been renamed (e.g. "Smith Family" is never overwritten by the pronoun), or the two players' actual names ("Alice & Carol") once a team's seats are both named and its name is still a default — "We"/"They" reads confusingly once multiple devices are looking at the same shared game, so real names win once they're available.
- **Per-hand locking (was per-match).** Two devices can now freely enter different hands of the same shared match/game at once — a lock is claimed only for the specific hand someone currently has open for editing, not the whole match, removing the old "this match is locked — take over or just watch" detour.
- **Personal Stats (v1).** A "Stats" link in the History tab footer opens a cross-player leaderboard (sortable by Wins / Win % / Moons / Streak) and a per-player detail screen — record & win rate, championships, longest win streak, moons shot, games played, a recent-form strip (last 10 games), a win/loss bar, and a games-by-month chart. Entirely derived from existing `gameHistory` (rosters, winner, per-deal moon data, tournament championship flags) — no new storage, works retroactively on games already logged. Players are matched by trimmed/case-folded name, same normalization as the per-device name book.
- **Merge names in Stats.** A "Merge names…" link on the Stats leaderboard lets a player fold typo'd or nicknamed entries ("Daniel" → "Dan") into one canonical name, with an undo per merge. Stored as a per-device alias map (`somerset:dev-name-aliases`) that `computePlayerStats()` resolves through before bucketing games — fixes the "same person, different spelling" case entirely client-side, no account or network needed. Does **not** address the same person's games logged on a *different* device (see cross-device identity below) — that's a separate, harder problem.
- **Personal Stats v2 — more stat categories.** Added on top of the v1 stat sheet: **Best partner** (best joint record, min. 2 games together) and **Toughest opponent/"nemesis"** (worst record against, min. 2 meetings, shown in red) in a new "Partners & rivals" section; **Times set** and **Moon conversion rate** tiles alongside the existing Moons-shot tile; a **Current streak** tile (e.g. "W5") distinct from Longest streak; **Biggest win margin**, **Longest game**, and **Shortest game** (by hand count) in a new "Highlights" section; **Tournaments played** folded into the existing championship banner ("2 championships from 5 tournaments"); and a **Win rate by format** breakdown (casual vs. single/double-elim vs. round robin vs. best-of series), shown once a player has played more than one format. All still pure aggregation over `gameHistory` — no new storage.
- **Bug fixes from a full stress-test sweep.** A Playwright harness driving every game mode across local and synced devices (see `stress-test/`) turned up four bugs, all fixed:
  - **Duplicate History entry for a self-identified host.** Completing a shared casual game you're both playing and self-identified in could archive to your own History twice — once immediately on the winning deal, once again on the Continue tap — via two dedup mechanisms that didn't know about each other. `advanceSharedGame` now marks the match archived and retroactively badges the championship flag, the same way the bracket flow already did.
  - **Per-hand locks not always releasing.** Canceling a hand entry before ever advancing past Step 1 released the lock only in this device's memory, not in storage/Firebase, leaving other devices locked out of that hand for up to the 10-minute stale-lock timeout. `releaseLock` now persists, matching `claimLock`.
  - **Misleading "Couldn't connect" error when joining with a bad code.** The security rules deny reads to nonexistent tournament codes as a permission error rather than returning null, so the friendlier "No tournament found with that code." message was unreachable — every mistyped or expired code looked like a network problem. Also added an offline pre-check and a 10s timeout so a genuinely unreachable connection no longer leaves the Join sheet hanging with no feedback at all.
  - **Orphaned undo-toast.** Deleting a second deal or History entry within ~250ms of clicking Undo on a prior delete could leave a stray, invisible toast permanently stuck in the DOM.
- **Forced identification on shared games; persistent device-wide identity.** Shared sessions previously let a host or guest skip "which player are you?" entirely, so their stats/History silently never got attributed — now, once any player in a shared session is named, the identify sheet can't be dismissed without a decision ("I'm just spectating" is still a valid explicit opt-out). Identity is also promoted from a per-join-code setting to a persistent, device-wide default (`MY_DEVICE_NAME_KEY`): once identified anywhere, the same name is silently assumed for any future game — local or shared — with a matching named seat, which also activates the previously-dormant "We/They" viewer perspective for local play. A one-shot, dismissable "is this you?" nudge seeds the device identity the first time a local seat is named, and a manual "Playing as" editor (Display sheet + Game Options) is the escape route to fix, set, or clear it by hand.
- **Cloud Backup & device linking.** Opt-in (☰ → Cloud Backup) sync of a device's Game History to Firebase, keyed on a stable anonymous Auth uid rather than a device ID. A separate "link this device" flow (reusing the existing tournament join-code UX) lets a second device redeem a short code and join the same `personId` group, so Personal Stats merges History across every device one person plays from — the real fix for the old "a device ID identifies a phone, not a person" gap, since `computePlayerStats()` already operated generically over `gameHistory` and just needed the union of a person's records. Hardened by a same-day security review (see `SECURITY_REVIEW.md`'s Addendum and companion correctness findings).
- **Stats Sharing (follow people you play with).** Opt-in (☰ → Stats Sharing, requires Cloud Backup): once on, anyone you play a shared game with is followed automatically after the first match — their overall record (wins, moons, sets, streaks, recent form, career totals from every game they record anywhere) appears live on their Stats page on your device. Their highlight games (biggest win, nailbiter, comeback win, Hail Mary, longest/shortest game) are shared as full records, open hand-by-hand with a tap — the same interaction the local Stats page's Highlights rows have. Mutual but independently controlled per person (a toggle revokes access immediately, enforced by Firebase rules); names in shared games are a separate, explicit opt-in, off by default.
- **Same-name/different-person handling in Stats.** Two different people who happen to share a roster name (unlike a typo, which Merge already covers) no longer get silently folded into one Stats entry. Roster entry now blocks re-adding a name already used by a different tracked person in the same slot; a "Split name…" tool (the inverse of Merge) un-merges a name back into distinct people; and Stats Sharing's claim flow disambiguates which of several same-named people a shared profile belongs to when it's ambiguous.
